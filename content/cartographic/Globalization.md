## Globalization

### Introduction & Definition

- The official definition of "globalization" is the process by which businesses or other organizations develop international influence or start operating on an international scale.
- More simply, globalization refers to an open flow of information, technology, and goods among countries and consumers.
- This openness occurs through various relationships, from business, geopolitics, and technology to travel, culture, and media.
- Because the world is already so connected, most people don't notice globalization at work every single day.
- The world is getting smaller, and companies need to understand what this means for the future of doing business.
- Companies that don't embrace globalization risk losing a competitive advantage, which allows other businesses to take over new opportunities in the global marketplace.

### Impacts & Benefits of Globalization

- **1) Access**:
  - Globalization makes it easier than ever to access foreign culture, including food, movies, music, and art.
  - This free flow of people, goods, art, and information is the reason individuals can have Thai food delivered to their apartment while listening to a favorite UK-based artist or watching a Bollywood movie.
- **2) The Spread of Technology and Innovation**:
  - Many countries around the world remain constantly connected, so knowledge and technological advances travel quickly.
  - Because knowledge transfers fast, scientific advances made in Asia can be at work in the United States in a matter of days.
- **3) Lower Costs for Products**:
  - Globalization allows companies to find lower-cost ways to produce their products.
  - It increases global competition, which drives prices down and creates a larger variety of choices for consumers.
  - Lowered costs help people in both developing and already-developed countries live better on less money.
- **4) Higher Standards of Living Across the Globe**:
  - Developing nations experience an improved standard of living thanks to globalization.
  - According to the World Bank, extreme poverty decreased by 35% since 1990.
  - The target of the first Millennium Development Goal was to cut the 1990 poverty rate in half by 2015.
  - This goal was achieved five years ahead of schedule, in 2010.
  - Across the globe, nearly 1.1 billion people have moved out of extreme poverty since that time.
- **5) Access to New Markets**:
  - Businesses gain a great deal from globalization, including new customers and diverse revenue streams.
  - Companies interested in these benefits look for flexible and innovative ways to grow their business overseas.
  - International Professional Employer Organizations (PEOs) make it easier than ever to employ workers in other countries quickly and compliantly.
  - For many companies, there is no longer the need to establish a foreign entity to expand overseas.
- **6) Access to New Talent**:
  - Globalization allows companies to find new, specialized talent that is not available in their current market.
  - Globalization gives companies the opportunity to explore tech talent in booming markets such as Berlin or Stockholm, rather than Silicon Valley.
  - International PEOs allow companies to compliantly employ workers overseas without having to establish a legal entity, making global hiring easier than ever.

### Challenges & Obstacles of Globalization

- According to Velocity Global's 2020 State of Global Expansion™ Report: Technology Industry, tech leaders in the U.S. and UK face significant obstacles when taking companies global.
- **1) International Recruiting**:
  - 30% of U.S. and UK tech leaders cited international recruiting as their most common challenge.
  - Recruiting across borders creates unknowns, requiring HR teams to build plans to interview and vet candidates when thousands of miles separate them from headquarters.
  - Companies need to know the market's demands for salaries and benefits to make competitive offers.
  - HR teams must factor in challenges like time zones, cultural differences, and language barriers to find a good fit for the company.
- **2) Managing Employee Immigration**:
  - Immigration challenges cause internal headaches, which is why 28% of U.S. and UK tech leaders agreed it was one of their top challenges.
  - Immigration laws change often, and in some countries, it is extremely difficult to secure visas for employees who are foreign nationals.
  - The U.S. is getting stricter with granting H-1B visas, and Brexit makes the future of immigration to the UK uncertain.
- **3) Incurring Tariffs and Export Fees**:
  - 29% of U.S. and UK tech leaders agreed that incurring tariffs and export fees is a challenge for their global businesses.
  - For companies looking to sell products abroad, getting those items overseas can be expensive depending on the market.
- **4) Payroll and Compliance Challenges**:
  - Managing overseas payroll and maintaining compliance with changing employment and tax laws is a common global expansion obstacle.
  - This management task gets even more difficult if a company tries to manage operations in multiple markets.
- **5) Loss of Cultural Identity**:
  - While globalization has made foreign countries easier to access, it has also begun to meld unique societies together.
  - The success of certain cultures throughout the world has caused other cultures to emulate them, causing cultures to lose their distinctive features.
- **6) Foreign Worker Exploitation**:
  - Lower costs create tough competition that leads some companies to search for cheap labor sources.
  - Some western companies ship their production overseas to countries like China and Malaysia, where lax regulations make it easier to exploit workers.
- **7) Global Expansion Difficulties**:
  - Setting up a compliant overseas presence is difficult for businesses wanting to go global.
  - Taking the traditional route of setting up an entity requires substantial upfront capital, sometimes up to $20,000, and costs of $200,000 annually to maintain the business.
  - Global businesses must keep up with different and ever-changing labor laws and navigate new legal systems to avoid missteps that lead to severe financial and legal consequences.
- **8) Immigration Challenges and Local Job Loss**:
  - Political climates in the United States and Europe demonstrate different viewpoints on the results of globalization.
  - Many countries around the globe are tightening immigration rules, making it harder for immigrants to find jobs.
  - This rise in nationalism is mainly due to anger from the perception that foreigners fill domestic jobs or that companies move operations abroad to save money on labor costs.
