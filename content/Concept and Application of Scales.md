## Scale: Concepts and Applications

### Introduction to Scales

- Distances on a map are smaller than the corresponding distance on the actual ground.
- Maps always bear a definite proportion to the area shown on them.
- Scale is the proportion of the distance between any two points on the map, corresponding to the actual distance between these same two points on the ground.
- Scale is functionally a ratio of two distances: the map distance and the ground distance.
- Example: A scale statement of "one cm. to one km." means a length of 1 cm on the map corresponds to a distance of 1 km on the ground (1cm = 1km).
- The scale chosen primarily depends on:
  - The size of the area to be mapped
  - The amount of details to be shown
  - The size of the paper
- There are at least three methods by which a scale can be expressed:
  1. Statement of Scale
  2. Representative Fraction (R.F.)
  3. Graphical Scale

### Expression Methods of Scale

#### 1) Statement of Scale
- The scale of a map may be indicated in the form of a written statement.
- Example: A written statement stating "1 cm represents 10 km" means a map distance of 1 cm represents 10 km of the corresponding ground distance.
- It may be expressed in alternative systems of measurement, such as "1 inch represents 10 miles".
- It is the simplest of the three scale expression methods.
- Limitation: People who are familiar with only one measurement system may not understand a statement of scale given in another system.
- Limitation: If the map is photographically reduced or enlarged, the written statement of scale becomes redundant, and a new scale must be worked out.

##### Advantages of Linear Scale / Statement of Scale
- This scale is very easy to understand as the calculations are not complex.
- In case of enlargement or reduction of the map, the linear scale gets reduced or enlarged proportionately, keeping map distance and ground distance relationships true.
- The area of a map can be easily determined with the help of this scale.
- A short distance on a map can be measured accurately through this scale.

##### Disadvantages of Linear Scale / Statement of Scale
- Calculations required for drawing this scale are very complex.
- Designing the scale along with headings to make it presentable is a laborious and time-consuming process.

#### 2) Graphical or Bar Scale
- This scale type shows map distances and corresponding ground distances using a line bar marked with primary and secondary divisions.
- In some configurations, the bar scale readings may be shown specifically in miles and furlongs.
- Like the statement method, it finds restricted use for only those who understand the specific unit system presented.
- Unique Advantage: Unlike the statement of scale method, the graphical scale remains completely valid when the map is reduced or enlarged.

##### Advantages of Graphical Scales
- Distances can easily be measured on the map with the help of a linear scale.
- It is easy to convert a verbal statement and a representative fraction into a graphical scale.
- It is easy to use for direct measurement of distance on the map.
- It remains true and valid even after the map has been photographically reduced or enlarged.

##### Disadvantages of Graphical Scales
- The scale can only be used by individuals who are familiar with the unit of measurement present on the scale.
- It requires time and proficiency to correctly create a linear scale.

#### 3) Representative Fraction (R.F.)
- R.F. shows the relationship between map distance and corresponding ground distance explicitly in units of length.
- The use of units to express the scale makes it the most versatile method.
- R.F. is generally shown as a fraction because it shows how much the real world is reduced to fit on the map.
- Example: A fraction of 1:24,000 shows that one unit of length on the map represents 24,000 of the exact same units on the ground.
- Example context: 1 mm, 1 cm, or 1 inch on the map represents 24,000 mm, 24,000 cm, or 24,000 inches, respectively, on the ground.

##### Advantages of Representative Fraction (R.F.)
- The use of units to express the scale makes it the most versatile method.
- Expressing the scale in units makes R.F. a universally acceptable and usable method.
- It is a unitless form of scale, allowing users to easily convert from one unit system to another.
- It is easily convertible.

##### Disadvantages of Representative Fraction (R.F.)
- It is only a fraction in which no specific system of units is visually used, meaning distances cannot be directly measured from it without calculation.
- Whenever a map is photographically enlarged or reduced, the printed R.F. value will no longer be true.

### Types of Graphical Scales

#### 1) Simple or Plain Scales
- They read or measure up to two units, or a single unit and its sub-division (e.g., centimeters (cm) and millimeters (mm)).
- They are used when measurements are required up to the first decimal place (e.g., 2.3 m or 4.6 cm).
- It consists of a line divided into a number of equal main parts, where the first main part is further sub-divided into smaller parts.
- Construction layout: Zero (0) is marked at the end of the first main part. Numbers are marked for main parts/units extending towards the right from the zero mark, and numbers for sub-divisions/smaller parts extend towards the left.

##### Advantages of Simple or Plain Scales
- Distances can easily be measured with the help of the linear scale.
- If the map is photographically enlarged or reduced, the linear scale is also altered in the exact same ratio and remains completely true to the map.

##### Disadvantages of Simple or Plain Scales
- Like the statement of scale, this scale can only be used by those who are familiar with the specific unit of measurement used.
- It requires time and proficiency to draw a graphic scale properly.

#### 2) Comparative Scale
- A comparative scale is a pair of scales sharing a common R.F. but graduated to read entirely different units.
- Example: A map drawn with a scale reading miles and furlongs can be measured off directly in kilometers and hectometers using a comparative scale.

##### Advantages of Comparative Scales
- Small differences between values can be measured.
- It utilizes the same known baseline.
- It is easily applied.
- It involves fewer steps or processes.
- It tends to prevent carryover errors from one judgment to another.
- It allows users to infer values beyond the specific stimulus objects scaled.

#### 3) Diagonal Scales
- Diagonal scales are used to read or measure up to three distinct units.
- Example units: decimeters (dm), centimeters (cm), and millimeters (mm); or miles, furlongs, and yards.
- This scale is specifically used when very small distances (such as 0.1 mm) must be accurately measured, or when measurements are required up to the second decimal place.

##### Advantages of Diagonal Scales
- It allows for the measurement of very small fractions of measurements, which are not possible with standard linear scales.
- While fractional measurements normally require various mathematical calculations, a diagonal scale allows direct measurement of fractions without further mathematical calculations.

##### Disadvantages of Diagonal Scales
- The primary disadvantage of the diagonal scale is the complexity of the measurement process itself.
- The measuring process with a standard linear scale is far easier.

#### 4) Vernier Scale
- The vernier scale is a device formulated by B.P. Vernier by which the fractional part of the smallest division of a main scale can be measured with the highest precision.
- It consists of a small auxiliary scale, called the vernier scale, which moves freely with its graduated edge along a fixed scale called the main scale.

##### Advantages of Vernier Scales
- The scale can be drawn plainly as a straight linear graph.
- The vernier carries an index mark that denotes the vernier division, which is indicated by an arrow mark.
- It is graphically and physically used on instruments like the Theodolite.

##### Disadvantages of Vernier Scales
- The data possesses an ordinal nature.
- It suffers from an inability to generalize beyond the specific stimulus objects scaled.

##### Vernier Constant Definition
- The difference between the value of one main scale division and the value of one vernier scale division is known as the least count of the vernier.
- This least count value is also known explicitly as the vernier constant.
