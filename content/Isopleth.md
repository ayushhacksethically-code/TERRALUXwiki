## Isopleth Mapping

### General Definition and System

- The term isopleth has been derived from the words "isos" meaning 'same' and "plethron" meaning 'measure'.
- Isopleths are lines joining places of equal values, which may be in the form of quantity, intensity, or density.
- Specific types of isopleths:
  - **Isobar:** A line representing points of equal atmospheric pressure.
  - **Isobath:** A line representing points of equal depth under water.
  - **Isobathytherm:** A line representing depths of water with equal temperature.
  - **Isochasm:** A line representing points of equal recurrence of auroras.
  - **Isocheim:** A line representing points of equal mean winter temperature.
  - **Isochrone:** A line representing points of equal time-distance from a point, such as the transportation time from a particular point.
  - **Isodapane:** A line representing points of equal transport costs for products from production to markets.
  - **Isodose:** A line representing points of equal intensity of radiation.
  - **Isodrosotherm:** A line representing points of equal dew point.
  - **Isogeotherm:** A line representing points of equal mean temperature.
  - **Isogloss:** A line separating linguistic features.
  - **Isogonal:** A line representing points of equal magnetic declination.
  - **Isohaline:** A line representing points of equal salinity in the ocean.
  - **Isohel:** A line representing points receiving equal amounts of sunshine.
  - **Isohume:** A line representing points of equal humidity.
  - **Isohyet:** A line representing points of equal precipitation.
  - **Isoneph:** A line representing points of equal amounts of cloud cover.
  - **Isopectic:** A line representing points where ice begins to form at the same time each fall or winter.
  - **Isophene:** A line representing points where biological events occur at the same time, such as crops flowering.
  - **Isoplat:** A line representing points of equal acidity, as in acid precipitation.
  - **Isopleth:** A line representing points of equal numerical value, such as population.
  - **Isopor:** A line representing points of equal annual change in magnetic declination.
  - **Isostere:** A line representing points of equal atmospheric density.
  - **Isotac:** A line representing points where ice begins to melt at the same time each spring.
  - **Isotach:** A line representing points of equal wind speed.
  - **Isothere:** A line representing points of equal mean summer temperature.
  - **Isotherm:** A line representing points of equal temperature.
  - **Isotim:** A line representing points of equal transport costs from the source of a raw material.

### Construction of Isopleth Maps

- Construction requires a base map of the area with point data showing values.
- All points with available data must be fixed on the map; a larger number of points yields a more accurate isopleth map.
- **Procedure 1:** Mark observed values accurately on the base map at the exact position data was collected, locating as many points as possible.
- **Procedure 2:** Decide the suitable value or number for the isolines by looking at the collected range, working out how many lines fit between maximum and minimum values using a fixed interval.
- **Procedure 3:** Join all points of equal value with a smooth line in accordance with the chosen interval, using "logical interpolation" to determine intermediate values.
- **Procedure 4:** Check the accuracy of construction quickly to ensure no points are omitted.
- **Procedure 5:** Show an appropriate key and title to assist interpretation; a key is always used when different shades or colors are applied.
- **Choosing the Interval:** Isopleths are drawn at a fixed interval where the interval between all lines must be the same.
- Close lines indicate a steep gradient, while distant lines indicate a gentle gradient.
- **Interval Selection Rules:**
  1. Determine the minimum and maximum values on the map.
  2. Calculate the range ($\text{Range} = \text{maximum value} - \text{minimum value}$).
  3. Based on the range, determine the interval in numbers like 5, 10, 15, etc.
  4. The exact point of drawing an isopleth is determined by a formula.

### Merits and Demerits

#### Isopleth Technique
- **Advantages:**
  - More scientific than other distribution methods and effectively shows distributions and variations.
  - Especially useful for climatic maps (isobars, isotherms, isohyets), serving as the main tool for meteorologists.
  - Extremely easy to determine the gradient or rate of change: wide-apart lines show a low gradient, while closer lines show a high gradient.
  - Lines are independent of political boundaries and best suited to show the natural pattern of distribution of an element.
  - Most suited method for showing elements having transitional values, which is why they are invariably used for temperature, pressure, and climatic elements.
- **Disadvantages:**
  - Many lines are drawn by interpolation, which can lead to inaccuracy in directions and distort the shown pattern of distribution.
  - It becomes confusing when there is a large list of points or dots.
  - It is time-consuming when dealing with a large number of dots or points.
