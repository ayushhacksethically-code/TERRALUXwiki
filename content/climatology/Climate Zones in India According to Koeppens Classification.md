### Introduction

- Wladimir Köppen (1846-1940) was born in Russia and was a great German Climatologist as well as an amateur botanist.
- His first-ever climate classification was presented in 1918, and he continued to refine and modify it until the last version was published in 1936.
- This classification uses different symbols to represent specific meanings and climates.
- Unlike Dudley Stamp, the Koeppen Classification is based on the monthly values of both temperature and precipitation.
- Wladimir Koeppen demarcated five major climate zones in India, which are further subdivided into specific sub-types.

#### Five Major Climate Regions According to Koeppen

1. Tropical Climate
2. Dry Climate
3. Warm Temperate Climate
4. Cold Temperate Climate
5. Ice Climate

#### The 9 Sub-Climate Regions of India According to Koeppen

| Symbol | Climate Type / Description | Geographical Location & Characteristics |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Amw** | Monsoon type with short dry winter season | Found in the western coastal regions of India and south of Goa; receives an average annual rainfall of about 300cm in summer during the south-west monsoon. |
| **As** | Monsoon type with dry season in summer | Rainfall occurs during the winters and summers are dry; receives an average annual rainfall between 75cm to 100cm during the retreating monsoon; lies in the Coromandel coast of Tamil Nadu. |
| **Aw** | Tropical Savanna type | Found in parts of the peninsular plateau; receives an average rainfall of about 75cm during the south-west monsoon; winters are dry. |
| **Bshw** | Semi-Arid Steppe type | Found in north-western Gujarat, parts of Western Rajasthan, and Punjab; average rainfall ranges between 12cm to 25cm; features arid-steppe vegetation (mainly fodder and grasses for cattle). |
| **Bwhw** | Hot Desert type | Found in parts of extreme western Rajasthan with a predominance of deserts; natural vegetation is almost absent due to excessive temperature; annual rainfall is less than 12cm. |
| **Cwg** | Monsoon type with dry winters | Found in most parts of the Ganga Plain, Eastern Rajasthan, Northern Madhya Pradesh, and most of north-east India; summer temperature is about $40^{\circ}C$ and winter temperature is about $27^{\circ}C$; most rainfall (100cm to 120cm) occurs in summer during the onset of the south-west monsoon. |
| **Dfc** | Cold, humid winter with short summer | Found in Arunachal Pradesh, Sikkim, and parts of Assam; winters are cold, humid, and longer; average annual rainfall is more than 200cm; average winter temperature is about $10^{\circ}C$. |
| **Et** | Tundra type | Found in the mountainous regions of Uttarakhand; average temperature is about $0^{\circ}C$ to $10^{\circ}C$; temperature falls with increasing altitude. |
| **E** | Polar type | Found in the states of Uttarakhand, Himachal Pradesh, and the Union Territory of Jammu & Kashmir; precipitation occurs as snow due to extremely low temperatures; temperature of the warmest month ranges from $0^{\circ}C$ to $10^{\circ}C$. |
