```mermaid

    Intro --> Intro_Bullets["• Arabic Word: 'mawsim' (meaning season).<br>• Definition: Seasonal periodic winds that reverse direction with the change of season.<br>• Path: Sea to land in summer, land to sea in winter (double system of seasonal winds).<br>• Locations: Pronounced in Indian subcontinent, central-western Africa, Southeast Asia."]

    Theories --> Theories_Bullets["• Classical Theory: Differential heating of land and sea (oldest explanation).<br>• Modern Theories: Air mass theory and jet stream theory.<br>• Indirect Global Factors: Southern oscillation, El-Niño, and La-Niña (contrary phase to El-Niño, harbinger of heavy monsoon in India)."]

    Flow --> Flow_Bullets["• Summer Monsoon (SW): Moist air moves northward from Indian Ocean. Winds originate in southern Indian Ocean high pressure, cross the equator, and gather abundant moisture that fuels convection & storm clouds.<br>• Winter Monsoon (NE): Cool, dry air moves southward from the subcontinent.<br>• Northeast Monsoon: Short duration; brings moisture from Bay of Bengal to southeast coastal states of India."]
```

## 3. Monsoon Impacts on India

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    Title["MONSOON IMPACTS ON INDIA"]
    Title --> Agri["1. Agriculture & Food Security"]
    Title --> Econ["2. Economy & Water Resources"]
    Title --> DisHealth["3. Disasters, Health & Culture"]
    Title --> Statistics["4. Statistics & Crops"]

    Agri --> Agri_Bullets["• Rely: SW summer monsoon brings the majority of India's annual rainfall.<br>• Crops: Rice, cotton, sugarcane, pulses.<br>• Risk: Deficient monsoon causes drought, crop failures, and threatens food security for millions."]

    Econ --> Econ_Bullets["• Economy: Agricultural performance directly impacts India's economy; strong rains boost rural income & demand; poor rains cause inflation & economic decline.<br>• Water Resources: Fills reservoirs, rivers, and groundwater aquifers for irrigation, drinking, and industry.<br>• Energy: Hydropower generation relies on filled reservoirs to meet national energy demands."]

    DisHealth --> DH_Bullets["• Disasters: Excessive rain causes floods and landslides, damaging property, infrastructure, and standing crops.<br>• Health: Water stagnation & poor sanitation lead to waterborne and vector-borne diseases (malaria, dengue).<br>• Culture: Celebrated with traditional festivals as relief from summer heat."]

    Statistics --> Stat_Bullets["• Volume: India receives ~70% of total annual rainfall during the monsoon season.<br>• Kharif Crops: Summer crops (rice, pulses, oilseeds/soybeans) are planted starting with June rains.<br>• Reversal: Blows from NE in cool months; reverses to SW in warmest months."]
```
