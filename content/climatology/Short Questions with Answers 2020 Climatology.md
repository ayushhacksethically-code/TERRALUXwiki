### 1) Break of Monsoon

- A Monsoon Break occurs when there are few or no rains for a few days during the rainy season.
- Monsoons inherently provide rain for a few days and then dry out for a few days, creating alternating wet and dry spells.
- These specific intervening days of no rain are referred to as the monsoon break.
- Conversely, a *Burst of Monsoon* occurs when it rains constantly for many days with exceedingly intense rainfall.

### 2) Hygroscopic Nuclei

- Hygroscopic nuclei refer to particles or aerosols in the atmosphere that possess the ability to attract and hold water vapor.
- This attraction leads directly to the formation of cloud droplets.
- These nuclei serve as the essential initial sites around which water vapor condenses to form visible clouds.

### 3) Pilot Flight Preferences (Atmospheric Layers)

- Commercial pilots typically fly within the troposphere, which is the layer of the Earth's atmosphere where most weather phenomena occur.
- The troposphere extends from the Earth's surface up to an average altitude of about 8 to 15 kilometers (5 to 9 miles), depending on the location and season.
- Commercial airplanes, including those used for passenger flights, are designed specifically for efficient and safe operation within this lower part of the atmosphere.

### 4) Factors of Insolation

- Insolation (incoming solar radiation) is influenced by several local factors that determine the total solar energy received at a particular location on the Earth's surface.
- The five primary factors are:
  - a) Latitude
  - b) Time of Day
  - c) Seasonal Variation
  - d) Day Length
  - e) Earth-Sun Distance

### 5) ITCZS (Intertropical Convergence Zone)

- The Intertropical Convergence Zone (ITCZ) is a belt of converging trade winds and rising air that encircles the Earth near the Equator.
- The rising air inside this zone produces high cloudiness, frequent thunderstorms, and heavy rainfall.
- The doldrums—oceanic regions of calm surface air—occur directly within this zone.
- The ITCZ shifts north and south seasonally with the sun.
- Over the Indian Ocean, the ITCZ undergoes especially large seasonal shifts spanning $40^{\circ}\text{--}45^{\circ}$ of latitude.

### 6) Basis of Climatic Classification by Koppen

- Developed by German climatologist Wladimir Köppen, it is one of the most widely used systems for categorizing world climates.
- The system classifies global climates into distinct structural categories using a combination of observed temperature and precipitation patterns.

### 7) Potential Evapotranspiration (PET)

- Potential evapotranspiration (PET) is the absolute amount of evapotranspiration, or evaporation, that may occur if plenty of water is present in the soil, a water source, or plants.
- The actual amount of evapotranspiration is limited by the water source.
- PET metrics are used for water management in agriculture, building, and drainage studies.

### 8) Jet Stream Influence on the Indian Monsoon

- The **Westerly Jetstream** brings western cyclonic disturbances (which originate over the Mediterranean Sea) into the Indian subcontinent from the west and northwest during winter.
- An increase in the prevailing night temperature generally indicates an advance in the arrival of these cyclonic disturbances.
- An **Easterly Jetstream**, called the sub-tropical easterly jet stream, blows over peninsular India approximately over $14^{\circ}N$ during the summer months.
- Tropical cyclones occur during the monsoon as well as in October–November and are a part of this easterly flow, affecting the coastal regions of the country.

### 9) SMOG

- Smog is formed when smoke, dust, carbon monoxide, sulphur dioxide, and other chemical pollutants contaminate and color the atmospheric fog.
- Smog is a common occurrence in large cities and industrial areas.
- It directly affects the human respiratory system.
